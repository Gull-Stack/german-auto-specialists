Common Audi Repairs We Handle

Timing chain tensioner failure is one of the most well-known issues with 2.0T Audi engines (A4, A5, Q5). If you hear a rattling sound on startup, don't wait — this can cause catastrophic engine damage. We catch these early and fix them for significantly less than the dealer charges.

Carbon buildup on intake valves affects all direct-injection Audi engines. Symptoms include rough idle, misfires, and reduced power. Our walnut blasting service restores full performance and fuel economy.

Electrical gremlins — from window regulators to infotainment issues — are common as Audis age. Our diagnostic equipment reads every module in the car, pinpointing the exact fault without the guesswork.

Why Independent Audi Repair Saves You Money

Audi dealerships charge $150-$200/hour for labor. Our rates are significantly lower, and we don't pad the bill with unnecessary "recommended services." A typical Audi brake job that costs $800-$1,200 at the dealer? We do the same work, with the same quality parts, for 30-40% less.

Your Audi's warranty is not voided by using an independent shop. Federal law (Magnuson-Moss Warranty Act) protects your right to choose where you service your vehicle.
